# UX Designer Interview Question Bank

This document organizes high-frequency North American tech interview questions for a senior / staff-level UX, UI, or product designer with experience in large-scale consumer platforms, AI products, design systems, prototyping, and design leadership.

This condensed version prioritizes questions that are likely to affect interview outcomes. Similar questions have been merged or removed.

## Tags

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Self-positioning | Background, career positioning, role fit, transition story, strengths. |
| Product judgment | Problem framing, prioritization, product strategy, user-business trade-offs. |
| UX/UI craft | Information architecture, interaction design, UI quality, visual hierarchy, usability. |
| AI & emerging tech | AI product thinking, multimodal interaction, trust, LLM/search, emerging interaction models. |
| Systems thinking | Design systems, platform UX, reusable standards, consistency, scalable design infrastructure. |
| Data & impact | Metrics, experiments, validation, business impact, measurable outcomes. |
| Collaboration | Cross-functional work, stakeholder alignment, engineering collaboration, influence. |
| Leadership | Coaching, team management, feedback, quality bar, seniority, retrospectives. |
| Logistics | Work authorization, location, work model, compensation, timing, offer details. |

## 1. Recruiter Screen / HR Initial Screen

| # | Question | What They Are Checking | Tags |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Can you walk me through your background? | Whether the candidate can summarize experience clearly and relevantly. | Self-positioning |
| 2 | How would you describe your current job search? | Current status, target roles, and urgency. | Self-positioning, Logistics |
| 3 | What kinds of roles are you targeting? | Whether the candidate fits Product Designer, Senior, Staff, AI Product Designer, Design Systems, or Lead roles. | Self-positioning |
| 4 | Why are you interested in this role? | Motivation and role fit. | Self-positioning, Product judgment |
| 5 | Why are you looking for a new opportunity now? | Career transition story and whether there are red flags. | Self-positioning |
| 6 | Why did you leave Baidu? | Whether the career transition is coherent and professional. | Self-positioning |
| 7 | Why did you move to Canada? | Whether the relocation and career transition make sense. | Self-positioning, Logistics |
| 8 | How does your master's degree in Applied Computer Science relate to your design career? | Whether technical study strengthens design rather than repositioning the candidate as an engineer. | Self-positioning, AI & emerging tech |
| 9 | Are you authorized to work in Canada? | Work authorization and sponsorship needs. | Logistics |
| 10 | Are you open to remote, hybrid, or onsite work? | Work model fit. | Logistics |
| 11 | What are your compensation expectations? | Salary range and negotiation fit. | Logistics |
| 12 | Can you briefly explain the scale of Baidu App for a North American audience? | Ability to translate China-based experience into a locally understandable frame. | Self-positioning, Data & impact |
